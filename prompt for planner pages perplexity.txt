Use this master prompt to generate as many planner page ideas as you want, while keeping them practical, printable, and niche-specific.

Master prompt
Create 20 planner page ideas for people dealing with [TOPIC OR STRUGGLE].

For each page, provide:

A page title.

A one-sentence purpose.

5 to 8 sections or prompts to include on the page.

A short layout suggestion.

A note explaining why this page would be helpful for that audience.

Requirements:

Design all pages for easy printability on 8.5 x 11 paper.

Use high contrast, minimal clutter, large writing spaces, and clear headings.

Make the pages practical, supportive, and immediately usable.

Avoid overly decorative elements or tiny sections.

Keep the tone encouraging and realistic.

Make the pages feel helpful for people who struggle with overwhelm, low focus, stress, fatigue, or emotional difficulty.

Do not repeat page ideas.

Make the ideas specific to [TOPIC OR STRUGGLE], not generic planner pages.

Format the output as a numbered list.

Make the ideas suitable for Etsy printable products, PDF planners, or digital planners.

Optional add-on:
After listing the 20 pages, group them into 3 product bundle ideas with bundle names and suggested page combinations.

Stronger version
Create 25 niche-specific planner page ideas for people who struggle with [TOPIC].

For each planner page, include:

Title.

Purpose.

Suggested sections.

Layout style.

Why it works.

Make these pages:

ADHD-friendly in readability, even if the niche is not ADHD.

Low-clutter, high-contrast, and easy to use.

Designed for printable PDF planners and digital planner bundles.

Helpful for people who need emotional support, structure, or gentle accountability.

Commercially appealing for Etsy buyers.

Also:

Include a mix of daily pages, weekly pages, trackers, reflection pages, emergency support pages, and routine pages.

Avoid generic pages like simple calendars unless they are adapted to the niche.

Focus on real-life usefulness and emotional relief.

End by listing the 10 best pages to create first.

Best prompt if you want highly sellable ideas
Create 30 highly useful and commercially appealing planner page ideas for a printable planner designed for people struggling with [TOPIC].

For each page, include:

A strong page title buyers would understand quickly.

A short purpose statement.

The exact sections to place on the page.

A brief layout description for a clean printable design.

A sentence explaining why it is especially helpful.

Requirements:

The pages must feel specific, not generic.

They must support people with real daily challenges related to [TOPIC].

They must be simple enough to print at home.

They must use large sections, strong headings, and minimal visual clutter.

They must be suitable for Etsy, printable bundles, and digital planning products.

Include a balance of planning pages, trackers, emotional support pages, and practical worksheets.

Avoid duplicates or weak filler ideas.

Prioritize pages that people would actually use repeatedly.

Then provide:

The best 10 pages for a starter bundle.

The best 10 pages for a premium bundle.

5 strong bundle name ideas.

Quick improvement trick
Add one of these lines to the end of any prompt:

“Make these ideas feel premium and unique, not generic.”

“Write these as if they are for a best-selling Etsy planner bundle.”

“Make the pages supportive for hard days, not overly productivity-focused.”

“Include both emotional support pages and practical planning pages.”

“Keep the ideas easy for me to turn into Canva templates.”
